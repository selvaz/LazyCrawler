# Executive Digest – Same-Day Briefing

## Monetary Policy & Trade Tariffs
**Dominant theme; sentiment clearly negative.**

- **US imposes sweeping tariffs on 60 partners** (BBC, Clarin). Rates 10%–12.5% on UK, China, EU, Canada, Japan, India, plus 10% on Argentina. Replaces expired Section 122 levy after Supreme Court struck down prior regime. Rationale: forced labour / worker welfare. *Implication:* broad-based risk-off for EM and DM exporters; USD strength likely vs exposed currencies (CNY, MXN, KRW) but also vs EUR/GBP. Argentine products face uniform 10% – a relatively mild outcome given fears of higher sectoral rates. Expect retaliatory announcements within days.

## Geopolitical Risk & Commodities
**Sentiment: bullish for crude, stagflationary tail risk.**

- **Oil breaches $100/bbl** (BBC). First time since May. Brent +6% driven by escalation in Middle East – US intensifies strikes on Iran; Houthi attacks on Red Sea tankers. UK petrol prices rising. *Implication:* immediate upside for energy equities, negative for airline/maritime transport. Adds to inflation pressure at a time tariffs are already pushing import prices higher. For central banks, this complicates the rate-cut narrative; yields may rise on inflation expectations despite growth slowdown risks.

## Emerging Markets / Local-Source Deep Dive
**Under-covered by Western wires:**

- **Argentina: trade deal expansion ahead of tariffs** (Clarin). Buenos Aires-based source reports that Argentina has secured EU deal ratification, with quotas for honey/rice already exhausted, and US beef sales up 5x plus tariff fluctuation protections. Country now trades with partners representing 33% of global GDP. Officials frame the tariff imposition as moderate compared to worst-case scenarios. *Why it matters:* suggests Argentina’s agri-export sector (soy, beef, grains) may be relatively resilient vs peers; peso-linked bonds could outperform EM high-yield on this differentiated access. Western media focused only on the 10% headline, not the negotiated carve-outs.

## Regional Flashpoints
- **Middle East oil supply disruption** remains the key non-tariff risk for global inflation and EM current accounts. Houthi Red Sea attacks persist – no de-escalation signal.

## Summary for Allocation
- **Rates:** bearish bias – tariffs + oil = higher near-term inflation prints; central banks likely to hold or delay cuts.
- **FX:** long USD (particularly vs EUR, GBP, CNY); watch ARS – tariff impact muted but still negative for risk.
- **Equities:** underweight broad EM ex-commodity producers; overweight energy; cautious on European/Asian exporters.
- **Commodities:** long crude, long agricultural (wheat/soy via Argentina/EU quota tightness).
